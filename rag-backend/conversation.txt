Assistant: Based on the provided context, there are two main types of sleep in humans and other mammalian species: Non-Rapid Eye Movement (NREM) sleep and Rapid Eye Movement (REM) sleep. Here's a breakdown of these sleep types and their functions:

1. Non-Rapid Eye Movement (NREM) Sleep:
   - NREM sleep is further divided into four stages:
     a. Stage 1: Light sleep, the transition between wakefulness and sleep.
     b. Stage 2: A deeper stage of light sleep, characterized by interesting brain rhythms.
     c. Stages 3 and 4: Deep sleep, also known as slow-wave sleep.

   Functions of NREM sleep:
   - Deep sleep (stages 3 and 4) is associated with brain cleaning and restoration from daily activities.
   - NREM sleep, particularly deep sleep, is important for memory consolidation and learning.

2. Rapid Eye Movement (REM) Sleep:
   - Characterized by rapid horizontal eye movements.
   - Also known as "dream sleep."
   - Some parts of the brain can be up to 30% more active during REM sleep than when awake.

   Functions of REM sleep:
   - Associated with dreaming.
   - Plays a role in memory consolidation, particularly for emotional and procedural memories.
   - Contributes to creativity and problem-solving.

Both types of sleep are essential and serve different purposes. Throughout the night, we cycle through these stages of sleep multiple times. The architecture of sleep (how these stages unfold across the night) is not only intellectually interesting but also has practical impacts on our daily lives.

It's worth noting that sleep is an active state, not a passive one. The brain undergoes significant changes during sleep, with dramatic shifts in brainwave activity that are more pronounced than those observed during wakefulness.
